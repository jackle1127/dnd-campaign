## Backstory

Frostguard is cold most of the year and dark half of it. People learn early to keep themselves entertained or go quietly mad. Fib chose music. By fifty he was trading songs for supper at every longhouse from Frostguard to Frosthaven, which suited him fine.

He spent decades on that circuit, playing wherever they'd have him. He got good at listening. Merchants talk when the ale is flowing, and the ale flows when someone's playing. Over the years he filled three journals: trade disputes, old feuds, debts owed to people long dead. Things said between songs that people probably hadn't meant to say at all.

He headed south without a particular reason. Old enough to be curious, young enough to still care. He crossed through Tripoint into the Heartlands, found the weather more agreeable, and kept moving. There's a decent inn at Ironwood Fortress, or so he's heard.

## The Lute of Drawn Tongues

The last elder at Frostguard's longhouse pressed a lute into his hands the morning he left. Dark wood - the kind that looks like it's been at the bottom of something cold for a very long time. The elder said it had passed through a dozen hands before his, always finding someone with a gift for listening.

Fib assumed the gift was his. When he played, people leaned forward and said things they hadn't planned to. Merchants told him about partners they didn't trust. Soldiers described orders they were supposed to carry quietly. It wasn't charm or coercion - the music simply opened something. His journals filled fast.

He woke one morning at a roadside inn between Tripoint and the Heartlands and it was gone. No forced door. Nothing else missing. Just the lute.

He's been playing on a borrowed instrument since. The music works. It just doesn't *work*. People are polite. They don't lean forward.

## The Third Journal

The last night he had the lute, he was sitting in a back corner of the Waypost Inn in Tripoint when two well-dressed strangers started arguing two tables over. One said: *If Dendros finds out the shipment never reached Vaultspire, we're all dead.* The other slid a sealed document across the table without a word. Fib noted it in his third journal, as he always did.

Since crossing into the Heartlands, he's had the sense of being followed. A man in a gray cloak. Never close enough to approach, never far enough to ignore.

Someone knows what the lute does. They watched him long enough to understand why the journals matter. Whether they wanted the lute for themselves, or wanted him disarmed first - he hasn't decided which is worse.
