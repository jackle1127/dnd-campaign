# Darian Thornecrest - Character Doc

## Darian Thornecrest - Backstory

Darian Thornecrest is the eldest son of House Thornecrest and the family's golden heir - everything his brother Kaelen was supposed to be and wasn't. Where Kaelen burned bright and chaotic, Darian was steady, polished, and exactly what a Thornecrest was meant to look like.

Growing up, Darian had every advantage handed to him: private tutors, the finest clothes, the best seat at every table. He didn't resent these gifts - he expected them. That's simply what it meant to be the heir. He was enrolled at Arcanum Lyceum in Bramblewood before Kaelen was old enough to cause trouble, and he thrived there. Top marks, popular among students and professors alike, elected to the academy's prestigious Honor Council. He graduated with distinction and returned to Bramblewood a certified wizard and the pride of House Thornecrest.

He never had to fight for anything. That's also his blind spot.

Darian is brilliantly intelligent - analytically, magically, politically. He can read a room, solve a problem, and craft an argument with equal precision. But wisdom? Lived experience? The kind of judgment that comes from actually suffering consequences? He has almost none. Everything has always worked out, because everything has always been arranged to work out. He doesn't know what it feels like to lose something that matters.

He's about to find out.

## The Mission

When Lord Garran Thornecrest discovered that his youngest son had stolen the family's Bag of Holding - and worse, that the sensitive documents inside were somewhere out there with it - he didn't panic. He dispatched Darian.

The mission was simple, at least on paper: find Kaelen, retrieve the bag, and handle the situation quietly. Lord Garran was characteristically cold about it. "Your brother made a mess. Clean it up."

Darian accepted without hesitation. Not out of brotherly concern - he told himself this was about the family, about responsibility, about being the one who actually shows up when things go wrong. He tracked Kaelen's trail for months, following whispers from Bramblewood to Sea Mist to Vaultspire and finally here.

He arrived flush with Thornecrest gold - a generous travel allowance from his father, more coin than most people see in a year.

He does not currently have that coin. (The circumstances remain to be determined.)

He doesn't know Kaelen is dead. He doesn't know what he's walking into.

## What He Won't Say Out Loud

Darian and Kaelen were never close. Darian was the responsible one, which meant Kaelen was the irresponsible one, which meant Darian spent most of their childhood quietly resenting being compared to his brother's chaos. He told himself for years that he didn't care what Kaelen did.

But he tracked him for months. He didn't have to do it personally - Lord Garran has agents for that. Darian chose to come himself.

He won't admit why. He barely admits it to himself. But somewhere under the arrogance and the family duty and the carefully maintained composure, there is a version of Darian that just wanted to find his brother before something bad happened.

He was too late.

When he learns that Kaelen died without his Mage Armor up - a spell they both learned in their second year - his reaction will be sharp and dismissive. He'll call it careless. He'll say something cutting.

He'll mean: I should have found you sooner.

## The Crew

Darian has no interest in the crew. They're criminals, misfits, the kind of people his family employs to move cargo and then never invites inside. He is not accustomed to needing anyone, and he is especially not accustomed to needing people like this.

But the Bag of Holding is gone - likely back at Vaultspire - and these people apparently know things he doesn't. They've also, it turns out, lost something of their own. The heirloom pieces. Which means for once in Darian's life, he needs allies he didn't choose.

He won't say "please." He won't say "I need your help." He will say something like: "It seems we want the same thing. Let's not waste each other's time."

He will have to earn their trust. He doesn't think he will. He also doesn't realize yet how much that will matter to him.

## Personality

The arrogant side: Darian leads with confidence that borders on condescension. He assumes he's the smartest person in the room - and is often right, which makes it worse. He corrects people. He sighs when things are done inefficiently. He has opinions about everything, including things that are none of his business.

The intelligent side: Darian is genuinely, formidably clever. He thinks fast, reads situations accurately, and has a memory like a steel trap. When he actually applies himself to a problem instead of just dismissing it, he's remarkable.

The not-very-wise side: He has no instinct for people. He can't tell when he's gone too far, can't read what someone actually needs versus what they're saying, and consistently underestimates how much his words land. He's been sheltered enough that consequences still feel abstract to him.

The gutted side: Kaelen was the only person in his family who ever made Darian feel anything unscripted. He misses him and has absolutely no idea how to process that.

## Family of House Thornecrest
### Lord Garran Thornecrest - The Patriarch
Cold, calculating patriarch. Sent Darian because it's efficient, not because he cares. Doesn't know and doesn't need to know that Darian has complicated feelings about any of this.
### Lady Merisse Thornecrest - The Matriarch
Quietly heartbroken about Kaelen, maintains perfect appearances. Slipped Darian an extra letter before he left. He hasn't opened it yet.
### Darian Thornecrest - The Heir
The eldest son and pride of House Thornecrest. Brilliant, disciplined, and exactly what a Thornecrest is supposed to look like. Carries the weight of the family's expectations without acknowledging it as weight.
### Liora Thornecrest - The Exile
The monk. Left the family. Darian respects her more than he lets on.
### Kaelen "Slick" Thornecrest - The Outcast
Deceased. Darian doesn't know yet.

## TL;DR for the DM
Darian is the golden heir of House Thornecrest - brilliant, arrogant, and completely unprepared for the real world. He was sent to quietly retrieve his brother and the Bag of Holding (which contains sensitive documents that could embarrass the family). He arrived with a lot of Thornecrest gold. He no longer has it.
He doesn't know Kaelen is dead yet. When he finds out, he won't show it - but it will break something in him. He blames himself without knowing he blames himself.
He needs the crew more than he will ever admit. He will be condescending to them. He will be wrong about them. Over time he should come to realize that these people - criminals, misfits, nobody's idea of allies - are the first people he's ever had to actually earn.
His mother slipped him a letter before he left. He hasn't opened it. It's a good hook.
The documents in the Bag of Holding haven't surfaced yet. If they do, House Thornecrest is in serious trouble and Darian becomes both an asset and a liability to the crew.
Play him smart, play him cold, and let the cracks show slowly.
